Midjourney continues to push the boundaries of AI-generated art, enabling creators to transform simple text prompts into vivid, imaginative visuals. If you truly want to unlock the potential of Midjourney, you need to understand its parameters—powerful tools that shape the final output. In this updated guide, we'll cover the newest features and best practices for Midjourney v7.

---

## Understanding Midjourney Parameters

Parameters are additional instructions appended to your prompt to guide how the image is generated. From aspect ratios to advanced styles, parameters let you fine-tune the creation process. You can use multiple parameters at the end of a single prompt.

    /imagine prompt: A serene lake at sunset --ar 16:9 --q 2 --v 7

Here, we're using three parameters: `--aspect`, `--quality`, and `--version`. Each has a distinct impact on how the image is produced.

---

## Basic Parameters

### 1. Aspect Ratio (`--ar` or `--aspect`)

Defines the width-to-height ratio of the generated image. Version 7 supports wider aspect ratios than ever before, including up to 21:9 for ultra-wide landscapes.

    /imagine prompt: Cyberpunk cityscape --ar 21:9

### 2. Chaos (`--chaos`)

Controls the level of randomness and variety in the output. Higher values lead to more unusual, creative results. In version 7, the range now goes from 0 to 200.

    /imagine prompt: Abstract emotion --chaos 120

### 3. Quality (`--q` or `--quality`)

Sets the rendering quality and time. In v7, `--q` can be 0.25, 0.5, 1, 2, or 3, with higher values potentially increasing generation time and texture detail—at the possible cost of coherence.

    /imagine prompt: Detailed mountain landscape --q 3

### 4. Seed (`--seed`)

Seeds ensure reproducibility. Entering the same seed with the same prompt and parameters will yield similar results. Valid values: `0` to `4294967295`.

    /imagine prompt: Futuristic space station --seed 1234

### 5. Stop (`--stop`)

Finishes the job partway through the generation process, producing simpler, less-detailed images. Version 7 still uses `1` to `100`.

    /imagine prompt: Misty forest --stop 50

---

## Advanced Parameters

### 6. Style (`--style`)

Switches between distinctive model styles or specialized modes. In v7, new style options such as `--style raw`, `--style expressive`, or `--style scenic` can be used.

    /imagine prompt: Anime character --style raw --niji 7

### 7. Stylize (`--s` or `--stylize`)

Adjusts the intensity of Midjourney’s artistic flair. The default is `100`, and v7 supports values from `0` to `4000`. Higher values lead to more interpretive, artistic renders.

    /imagine prompt: Portrait of a wise old man --s 2000

### 8. Tile (`--tile`)

Produces seamless, repeatable patterns. Ideal for backgrounds or texture creation. As of v7, you can optionally use `--tile seamless` for additional refinements.

    /imagine prompt: Art deco pattern --tile seamless

### 9. Weird (`--weird` or `--w`)

Pushes your prompts into more experimental territory. In version 7, `--weird` can go up to `5000` for especially surreal results.

    /imagine prompt: Surreal dreamscape --w 3000

### 10. Version (`--v` or `--version`)

Specifies which Midjourney model version to use. Use `--v 7` to access the latest features and improvements.

    /imagine prompt: Futuristic cityscape --v 7

### 11. Uplight (`--uplight`)

Applies a lighter upscaling algorithm, cutting down on processing time but potentially sacrificing some detail.

    /imagine prompt: Elegant ballroom --uplight --v 7

### 12. No (`--no`)

Excludes certain elements or styles from your image.

    /imagine prompt: Fantasy dragon --no fire

### 13. Video (`--video`)

Provides a timelapse-like video of your image’s evolution. Keep in mind it may slightly extend generation time.

    /imagine prompt: Cute puppy playing --niji 7 --video

### 14. Coherent (`--coherent`)

New in v7, `--coherent` aims to maintain a stronger alignment with your prompt details, reducing random artifacts and tangential visuals. Try this for more literal interpretations.

    /imagine prompt: Vintage car showroom --coherent

## Tips for Effective Parameter Use

1. **Test Combinations**  
   Don’t hesitate to mix and match parameters (e.g., `--tile`, `--weird`, `--chaos`). Each combination can lead to a totally different look.

2. **Iterate with Seeds**  
   Use `--seed` to refine or replicate a prompt. Seeds help you isolate what’s changing and build on concepts you like.

3. **Balance Speed and Detail**  
   `--q 2` or `--q 3` can drastically increase detail but slow down generation. For quick concept tests, try `--q 0.5` or `--q 1`.

4. **Control Midjourney’s Style**  
   `--stylize` can dramatically transform or refine your images. Higher values yield more interpretive renders, while lower values adhere to your prompt more strictly.

5. **Try `--coherent` for Literal Prompts**  
   If you need exact representation of your text, use `--coherent` to reduce any unintentional or overly artistic deviations.
